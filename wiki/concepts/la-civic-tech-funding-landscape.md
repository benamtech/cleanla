Which foundations and grant programs actively write checks for civic technology in Los Angeles in 2026. Compiled to inform the institutional-form decision for [[../projects/cleanla-snap]] — specifically, whether grant runway is a realistic substitute for, or bridge to, formal 501(c)(3) ownership.

The short answer: **yes, grant runway is realistic**, but the candidate set is narrower than the obvious foundation names would suggest.

## The realistic shortlist

### 1. LA2050 (Goldhirsh Foundation) — highest probability, most accessible
Participatory grants challenge directing [$3M annually](https://la2050.org/) (2025: $3M from Goldhirsh + 11 partners including Broad, Conrad Hilton, Snap Foundation, OpenAI). Voting-driven — categories chosen by the community, not pre-set. Awards typically $20K-$100K+; 55 orgs funded in 2025. Timeline: voting (spring), org screening + Part 2 proposals (summer), awards October. Technology/communications solutions are eligible under community-voted priorities. **Next cycle: spring 2027.**

### 2. Annenberg Foundation (AlchemyTech / PledgeLA / LA2050 partner) — high probability
Explicitly funds LA civic tech + media literacy. Launched [AnnenbergTech and PledgeLA](https://annenberg.org/news/mayor-eric-garcetti-annenberg-foundation-and-la-tech-leaders-launch-unprecedented-civic-initiative/) with the City of LA. Funds LA2050 with an [AlchemyTech track for tech and communications solutions](https://annenberg.org/news/alchemytech-my-la2050-challenge-winners-announced/). Grants $5K-$100K+.

### 3. Mozilla Foundation Democracy x AI Cohort — medium probability, high value
[$1M total, $50K-$300K per team](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-do/grantmaking/incubator/democracy-ai-cohort/) for technology addressing institutional transparency, civic space, information systems. **Requires a working prototype, not just an idea.** US 501(c)(3) or non-US equivalent eligible. 2026 cycle: deadline March 16, winners June. Strong fit if CleanLA Snap has a functioning MVP by next cycle.

### 4. Schmidt Futures / Center for Civic Futures — medium probability, conditional fit
$500K-$1M+ via the [AI for Public Benefits Access initiative ($8.5M committed)](https://www.centerforcivicfutures.org/resources/center-for-civic-futures-and-partners-commit-8-5m-for-ai-solutions-that-improve-safety-net-program-delivery/). Focus is safety-net / benefits-access / caseworker tools. Direct fit only if CleanLA Snap evolves to integrate with LA's 311 or service-delivery systems (vs. pure citizen reporting).

### 5. Community Partners LA (fiscal sponsor) — immediate path
Not a funder, but the standout local fiscal sponsor. [9% fee on private grants, 15% on government grants](https://communitypartners.org/fiscal-sponsorship/), 4-8 week approval, $2K minimum annual fee. Enables grant-ready status before forming a 501(c)(3). De-risks the institutional-form decision: sponsor now, apply to the above funders, decide incorporation strategy based on grant commitments by fall 2026.

## Confirmed candidates not on the shortlist (and why)

- **Knight Foundation Cities Challenge** ($200K per project, [link](https://knightfoundation.org/knight-cities-challenge/)) — LA is not on Knight's 26 priority newspaper-cities list. Skip unless LA gets added.
- **Ballmer Group** — [does prioritize LA County](https://ballmergroup.org/our-work/), has a Data & Technology team, but the visible grants are infrastructure-level partnerships with established LA County orgs, not seed-stage civic tools. Worth a pitch only after CleanLA Snap has institutional momentum.
- **California Endowment** — $4.1B health foundation. Civic tech is not a stated priority. Health-equity frame only.
- **James Irvine Foundation** — [shifted to low-income workers / statewide economic opportunity](https://www.irvine.org/). Civic tech is not a current program area.
- **Patrick J. McGovern Foundation** — [funds AI/data civic tech globally](https://www.mcgovern.org/2025-press-release/) (75.8M across 149 orgs in 2025), but grantees are usually institutional partners (governments, academia). Less of a fit for a small consumer app.
- **Skoll Foundation** — [$1.25M awards to mature social-entrepreneurship orgs](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skoll-foundation-announces-winners-of-the-2026-skoll-award-for-social-innovation-302734797.html). Monitor for future, not for v1.

## Civic-tech intermediaries (not funders, but connectors)

- **[Hack for LA](https://www.hackforla.org/)** — Code for America brigade. Volunteer community, [publishes the Civic Tech Index](https://www.hackforla.org/projects/civic-tech-index.html). Critical for surfacing funding leads and building community credibility.
- **Civic Hall LA** — couldn't verify it exists as an active entity. The physical "Civic Center" in downtown LA returns search results but no civic-innovation hub.

## Fiscal-sponsorship alternatives to Community Partners LA

If Community Partners LA isn't the right fit (mission mismatch, capacity, fees):

- **[Tides Foundation](https://www.tides.org/what-we-do/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 9% fee, 130+ projects, 4×/year approval cycle. National scope.
- **[NEO Philanthropy](https://neophilanthropy.org/services/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 6-10% fee, but by invitation only.
- **[Players Philanthropy Fund](https://ppf.org/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 6% overhead, 600+ projects, $5K minimum deposit.
- **[Multiplier](https://multiplier.org/)** — environmental/social-equity scope; only fits if aligned with UN SDGs.

## Grant-size expectations (rough ranges)

| Source | Range |
|---|---|
| LA2050 | $20K-$100K+ |
| Annenberg / AlchemyTech | $5K-$100K+ |
| Mozilla Democracy x AI | $50K-$300K |
| Schmidt / Center for Civic Futures | $500K-$1M+ |
| Knight Cities Challenge (if LA-eligible) | up to $200K |
| McGovern | $100K-$500K+ |
| Skoll Award | $1.25M (very selective) |

**Realistic Year-1 pipeline for an early-stage LA civic-tech project: $30K-$150K via LA2050 + Annenberg. Potential for $250K-$500K+ in Year 2 if traction is demonstrable.**

## Implications for [[../projects/cleanla-snap]]

- The institutional-form gap I flagged in the project page can be unblocked with a Community Partners LA fiscal sponsorship in 4-8 weeks, before incorporating a separate 501(c)(3). This decouples "have a legal home" from "decide between Naula's 501(c)(3), a new one, or a fiscal sponsor permanently."
- LA2050 + Annenberg is the realistic Year-1 funding pitch. Both are LA-focused, both value community endorsement, both reward visible product traction over institutional polish.
- The [[cleanlawithme-movement|Clean LA With Me]] partnership conversation is more valuable in this context: an LA2050 application backed by a high-profile 501(c)(3) co-applicant lands much harder than one from an unaffiliated team.
