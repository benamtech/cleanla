# Regional Demoscenes + Scene Magazines (Diskmags)

> **The Anglo-Finnish-German axis is not the whole demoscene.** Polish, Hungarian, Russian, Czech, Ukrainian, Romanian, Bulgarian scenes each have distinct histories — most under-documented in English. Scene magazines (diskmags) were the era's primary knowledge-preservation medium: Hugi (Austria, 1996+), Imphobia (Belgium, 1992), PAiN (Polish, 1994–2007), Bitfellas ZINE (post-2000). This canon documents what's findable in English; explicitly names native-language gaps.

For mainline demoscene narrative, see [[demoscene]]. For practitioner contact paths, see [[practitioner-network]]. For epistemic limits, see [[knowledge-bounds]].

## Scene Magazines (Diskmags) — the era's knowledge bottleneck

### Hugi (Austria/Germany, 1996–present)
- **Editor:** Adok (Claus D. Volko), Vienna. Started age 12 in 1996.
- **Issues:** 39+ documented (1996–2014+). Dual German/English from issue 11.
- **Technical:** Panorama engine (Polish programmer Chris Dragan); **first Windows diskmag** (Sept 1998).
- **Rankings:** Hornet Charts 1998 #2, Sunray 1999 #1, Planet 2001 #1, World 2002 #1, Savage 2006 #2 — five #1 chart positions over a decade
- **Archive:** hugi.scene.org (full online; DOSBox compatible for legacy issues)
- **ANSI/ASCII coverage:** "Diskmag Galore" articles (#36–37, Adok). "18 Years of Hugi — Looking Back" (#38). Interview specials.
- **Why important:** Longest-running active diskmag. Bridges 1990s diskmag tradition to 2010s online era.

### Imphobia (Belgium, 1992–96)
- **Editors:** Darkness (primary from #4); founders Wizard, Darkness, Scorpio, Zorlock (Belgium); team expanded: Sam (Germany), Numerus (Switzerland), Mop (Malta)
- **Issues:** 12 released
- **Significance:** **First European PC diskmag.** First diskmag of its kind (Sept 1991, released as "review in VGA").
- **Legacy:** Canonical influence on diskmag design/interface
- **Archive:** scene.org/browse/mags/imphobia/
- **Coverage in Hugi:** #35–#36 (Adok) detailed Imphobia's editorial history

### PAiN (Polish origin, 1994–2007)
- **Issues:** Spanning 13+ years; DOS → Windows
- **Member of:** The E-Mag Network (Defcon, CFX News, Armor of the Gods, Hugi)
- **Content:** Scene news, interviews, party reports; **graphically sophisticated**
- **Archive:** pain.scene.org + diskmag.conspiracy.hu/magazine/pain
- **Regional value:** Primary Polish-language demoscene source

### Bitfellas ZINE (post-2000, Brainstorm collaboration)
- **Issues:** ZINE #12, #14+ documented
- **Content:** Demoscene + digital arts
- **Archive:** zine.bitfellas.org
- **Companion platforms:**
  - **Artcity** — graphics archive
  - **Bitworld** — Amiga encyclopedia
- **Notable:** ZINE #14 is **the only English-language source** on Russian ZX Spectrum demoscene history

### Other named diskmags (limited search results)
- **Daskmig** (German, 1990s) — documented in Hugi histories; PC-focused
- **The Bottom Line (TBL)** — preserved in scene.org/mags
- **Twilight Magazine** — Norwegian/Danish
- **Showtime** — PC scene mag
- **Demo Journal** — various editors, 1990s
- **Vandal** (Russian) — editor + issue count unknown

### Diskmag aggregator infrastructure
- **diskmags.de** — 1,355+ magazines catalogued (merged from Internet Archive, Kultboy, Pouët, C-64 Scene Database, ZX Press)

## Polish Demoscene

### Peak years
1995–2010. **UNESCO intangible cultural heritage (2021).**

### Major groups
- **Mawi** (founded 1995, Czaplinek) — Seti (swapper/graphician), Heron (coder), Jacko (graphician), Komar (swapper)
- **Kakiarts** — graphician group (era undocumented in English sources)
- **Mad Wizards** — Azzaro (interviewed in Hugi)
- **Psykon, Quad, Asphyxia, Floppy, Beyond, Pyrotechnics** — peak-era groups
- **Mystic Bytes** (Atari demogroup, Poland, founded 1998) — organized Little Atari Convention 2004
- **Ubik** — Polish coder (interviewed in Hugi)

### Demoparties
- **Riverwash** (2007–2018) — Poland's biggest multiplatform party
  - Held in Szymovive (2007), Katowice (2018 final)
  - Created to revitalize scene after Symphony declined
- Earlier Polish parties: Symphony (predecessor), Adventure, X-Mas

### Documentation
- **PAiN diskmag (1994–2007)** — primary Polish-language source
- **Hugi #34** — "Polish Demoscene: Quo Vadis?" (Magic)
- English-language Hugi interviews: Ubik, Mad Wizards (Azzaro)
- **culture.pl** — official Polish culture portal documents UNESCO heritage status

## Hungarian Demoscene

### Peak years
2003–present (demoparty-centered).

### Major groups
- **Conspiracy (CNS)** — founded November 18, 2002. Core organizers of Function demoparty
  - **Chaos Theory** (2006)
  - **Darkness Lay Your Eyes Upon Me** (2016)
- **Digital Murder Productions** — 64K specialists
- **Astral Vibes** — graphics-focused
- **NaturalParty** — demogroup organizers
- **Mature Furk Award** — past compo winners

### Demoparties
- **Function** (2003–present) — biggest Hungarian demoscene event
  - Annual 3-day (September weekend), Budapest-based
  - **Year-by-year results documented at function.hu** (2018, 2021, 2023 sites active)
  - Function YouTube channel preserves talks + showings

### Documentation
- conspiracy.hu — Conspiracy group archives
- function.hu — Function demoparty
- 2023.function.hu — recent edition results
- Demozoo cross-listings

## Russian Demoscene

### Peak years
Early 1990s–present. **ZX Spectrum uniquely dominant.**

### ZX Spectrum dominance — the unique Russian story
- 1984–85 — Ukrainian engineers (Lvov) first cloned ZX Spectrum using Soviet parts
- Clones in most large cities by 1987
- Late 1980s–early 1990s — ZX Spectrum demoscene "slow to start" then **rose sharply**
- Russians claimed superiority over Polish coders (early 1990s)
- Soviet/CIS adoption: cult status equivalent to C64 in Western Europe
- **Most popular 8-bit home computer for CIS**

### Major groups
- Sage, NoName, Brainstorm, Antares, Synth, Cocoon
- **ZINE #14 (Bitfellas, 2006+):** "A Brief History of the Russian Spectrum Demoscene" — **primary English source**, only paper of its kind

### Demoparties
- **Chaos Constructions** (St. Petersburg, 1999–present) — largest ZX Spectrum party (post-ENLiGHT rebranding 1999); annual computer-art festival + IT conference
  - URL: c-c.ru
- **Funtop** (Moscow, Spectrum-only, historical)
- **Aurora** (Russian, undocumented detail)
- **DiHalt** (2005–present, undocumented detail)

### Critical gap
**Native-language Russian scene magazines** (Vandal, etc.) and BBS-era files largely inaccessible to non-Cyrillic researchers. The Russian scene is **the largest non-Western demoscene by production volume** yet near-invisible in English sources.

## ANSI/ASCII Art Coverage in Regional Diskmags

1. **Hugi** — "Diskmag Galore" articles (#36–37, Adok) review international diskmag design + editorial history; graphics + typography emphasized; no dedicated ANSI/ASCII section identified
2. **PAiN** — known graphically sophisticated; ANSI/ASCII coverage likely but unconfirmed
3. **Text Mode Demo Contest (TMDC)** — first held 1996; mid-1990s demoscene turn toward text-mode demos; parallel to diskmag culture but distinct
4. **ACiD / iCE / Dark Illustrated** — ANSI art groups distributed monthlies via BBS/FTP 1990s; separate from diskmag tradition but contemporary
5. **Bitfellas ZINE** — Amiga ASCII community coverage noted (online era, post-2000)

**Missing:** Specific Hugi issues with "best ANSI art" features. Polish/Hungarian/Russian ANSI documentation.
