Tactical prep for the CleanLA founder's first 30-60 minute conversation with Juan Naula of [[./cleanlawithme-movement|Clean LA With Me]], targeted for 2026-06-07. The deep partnership precedents and theory live in [[./civic-tech-founder-org-handoff-patterns]]; this page is the conversation-specific playbook.

Full research: `raw/0009-clwm-partnership-prep.md`.

## Posture before the call

**Frame yourself as an enabler of his 2026-2030 vision, not a replacement for his grassroots brand.** Naula built CLWM on authenticity, personal narrative, and direct Instagram connection. CleanLA's value is infrastructure that lets him scale to "100+ permanent cleanup hubs" (his stated 2026 goal) without losing the intimate, dignity-centered volunteer experience.

## State of CLWM as of 2026-05-24

- **Instagram followers:** ~50K (discrepancy with [[./cleanlawithme-movement]] which said ~69K — figure may be stale, volatile, or a measurement difference; verify in call)
- **Operating cadence:** weekly cleanups, 250+ volunteers per event, 1,500+ trash bags collected to date
- **Org structure:** 501(c)(3) status claimed but **NOT confirmed** via IRS lookup; no board public; likely 1-2 person org
- **Current tool stack:** Instagram + Google Forms + Giving Circles + GoFundMe. No CRM, no volunteer-management, no event platform, no Discord
- **Existing partnerships:** Mayor Bass public endorsement (State of the City Address); zero corporate sponsors; zero civic-tech partnerships
- **Reputational profile:** clean. No controversies. Dignity-forward, humanitarian framing. Two hit-and-runs during cleanups (Aug 2025) — safety/liability is a real conversation
- **Vision (his words):** "By 2026: 100+ permanent cleanup hubs with tools and local leadership. By 2030: Smart waste infrastructure with AI-powered systems"

## What to OFFER (frame early)

1. **Volunteer coordination** — replace the single Google Form with neighborhood-hub structure, sign-ups per team/date/role, repeat-volunteer tracking
2. **Impact data layer** — live cleanup mapping, weight tracking, exportable reports for donors/city/press
3. **Donor-volunteer integration** — unify Giving Circles + volunteer pipeline so donors see impact and volunteers can convert to donors
4. **Hub-scaling infrastructure** — neighborhood hubs each get team, leaders, mini-dashboards (his stated 2026 goal)

## What to ASK (learn before pitching)

- "You mention 100 hubs by 2026. What does that look like operationally?"
- "Your donors give to $10K Guinness campaigns — how do they know what happened to their money?"
- "Mayor Bass endorsed you. Have you explored formal partnership with Bureau of Street Services / Sanitation? What's blocking it?"
- "You have 250+ per event — how many are repeat volunteers? How do you keep them engaged?"
- "If we proposed a more integrated tool, what's your hesitation? Cost? Control? Over-professionalization?"

## What to AVOID

- "Your current setup is broken" — it's viral and working socially
- "We'll help you go corporate / get a board / hire staff" — not your role; he may reject professionalization
- Corporate partnerships / sponsorship pitches — zero appetite shown
- Encampment-clearing framing — his brand is dignity-first, not enforcement-adjacent
- Political alignment — he's apolitical; respect that
- Data extraction / surveillance optics — CleanLA-owned data, not city-controlled

## The critical question to ask (per [[./civic-tech-founder-org-handoff-patterns]])

> "Do you want to *own* this tool, or use a tool I've built?"

His answer determines the entire partnership structure (outright donation vs license vs co-ownership vs founder-as-CTO). Don't pre-decide.

## Gaps to close in the call

- 501(c)(3) confirmation (IRS EIN, Form 990, board composition)
- Volunteer retention data
- Co-leadership existence (Naula plus anyone else?)
- Safety / incident protocols (post-hit-and-run)
- Current city-relationship trajectory (talks with Bureau of Street Services?)
