# Deep ANSI / ASCII / BBS Archive Reference

> **26+ archives, 50+ named artists, 15+ distinct text-art traditions.** The standard three archives (16colo.rs, artscene.textfiles.com, breakintochat.com) are the doorway — but the comprehensive picture requires defacto2, demozoo, scene.org, csdb, the Wayback Machine for defunct group sites, plus parallel-tradition archives (NAPLPS/RIPscrip/Teletext/Minitel/PRESTEL). This page is the canonical archive locator for 369 ASCII / ANSI / BBS-art work.

For art-history narrative, see [[ascii-ansi-art]]. For the demoscene context, see [[demoscene]]. For niche parallel traditions, see [[niche-text-traditions]].

---

## Tier 1 Core Pillars (5 archives)

### 1. Defacto2.net
**URL:** https://defacto2.net/

The deepest PC-scene archive. **60,817+ preserved files.** Goes well beyond ANSI art into the full warez/scene context.

**Unique material:**
- ACiD Productions' **April 1992 *The Acquisition*** — the first regularly-released ANSI artpack series
- Named-pseudonym indexes: Phantom, Shadow Demon, RaD Man, The Beholder (all ACiD founders, Sept 1990)
- iCE Advertisements (Jan 1991)
- Remorse ASCII (Necromancer, Necronite, 1994–95)
- Aces of ANSI Art (AAA): Zyphril, Chips Ahoy, San Jose (1989–91) — the *first* underground elite ANSI group, predating ACiD

**Status:** Online, actively maintained. Source: github.com/Defacto2/server

### 2. 16colo.rs / Blocktronics hub
**URL:** https://16colo.rs/

Premier modern aggregator. Real-time color rendering of every preserved artpack from 1990 onward.

**Coverage as of 2026:**
- 10,000+ artworks
- Blocktronics: 43 artpacks, 2,513 lifetime artworks
- Continuous monthly releases through 2026
- Modern named profiles: aesthetic, illogic, zeebit, celestian
- Blocktronics directory tracks 2024 (1 release), 2025 (5 releases), 2026 (2 YTD)

**Why it matters:** The primary contemporary preservation platform. **The only archive where modern artists actively contribute.**

### 3. Scene.org Graphics Archive
**URL:** https://files.scene.org/browse/graphics/ascii/

European demoscene file-distribution hub. Organized by group + demoparty compo result.

**Unique material:**
- ASCII compos from Assembly, The Party, Mekka & Symposium, NAID, Forever (1990s–2010s)
- Group archives: ACiD, iCE, SAC, Remorse historical packs
- Mirrored globally (Northern European universities + ftp.scene.org)

### 4. Roysac.com — Superior Art Creations
**URL:** https://www.roysac.com/

Sam Roy's living museum + active tutorial site. **The reference for contemporary ANSI practitioners.**

**Unique material:**
- Halaster's 12-step shading method (full tutorial)
- Nootropic's ASCII font shading techniques
- Enzo's female character drawing tutorial
- SAC art packs Dec 1994 → Dec 2009 (15 years catalog)
- 100+ named-contributor index

### 5. Demozoo.org Groups Database
**URL:** https://demozoo.org/groups/

Structured metadata for **100+ ANSI art groups**. Wiki-style with interview citations.

**Unique material:**
- ACiD entry — detailed 1990–2004 timeline
- iCE entry — Frozen Tormentor interview claim of stability through turbulent era
- AAA entry — claims to "first underground elite ANSI group"
- Cross-links to Wayback for original group sites
- Demoparty result archives — ANSI compo winners by year

## Tier 2 Parallel Tradition Archives

### 6. Teletext Art (UK Ceefax Legacy)
**Primary:** https://teletextart.co.uk/ (TARL — Teletext Art Research Lab)

**Status:** Online; active 2024–2026.

**Coverage:**
- 1.8M+ teletext pages recovered from 100+ channels worldwide
- 25 teletext archivists
- 50 years domestic videotape recovery (1960s–2010s)
- NMS Ceefax live broadcast simulation: https://nmsceefax.co.uk/
- Ceefax (BBC, 1974–2012) — 38 years of active service
- viewdata.org.uk + teletext archaeology blogs

### 7. Minitel Archives (French Videotex)
**Primary:** https://www.goto10.fr/ (last refreshed June 2024)
**Forum:** https://forum.museeminitel.fr/ (Musée du Minitel)

**Coverage:**
- French BBS + Minitel archive (videotex / flashcode subdirectories)
- GitHub Videotex Pages Repository: github.com/XReyRobert/VideotexPagesRepository
- Academic revival projects (Auxerre students, 2019–2020)
- Distinct from NAPLPS but chronologically parallel videotex tradition

### 8. PRESTEL (BT Videotex)
**Sources:**
- en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PRESTEL
- Michael Aldrich Archive — aldricharchive.co.uk/videotex
- archive.org/details/Welcome-to-Prestel-Gateway

**Coverage:**
- UK's first public online service (Sept 1979)
- 450+ technical support forums by early 1990s
- Prestel Gateway (1982) linked external computers
- Documented home banking, online shopping, telesoftware delivery
- BT archives at archivesit.org.uk/bt/

### 9. CSDb — Commodore Scene Database
**URL:** https://csdb.dk/

The PETSCII archive. Most active 2024–2026 of any historic-platform scene.

**Coverage:**
- PETSCII competitions 2013–2026
- Plain PETSCII Graphics Compo (2013 onward)
- PETSCII Frame Contest (2024)
- TitleMaker Generator (2026, Vintage Computing Carinthia)
- 250+ PETSCII releases indexed

**Modern artists tracked:**
- Ko-Ko (*PETSCII Wizard*, 2026)
- Level90, Xenon, Pixel Punx, Abyss Connection

### 10. NAPLPS / Telidon
**Sources:**
- en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NAPLPS
- handwiki.org/wiki/NAPLPS
- UVic Library Subject Index: dspace.library.uvic.ca

**Coverage:**
- Bell Canada Telidon protocol (1979–94 Alex dial-up service)
- CSA T500 / ANSI X3.110 standardization (1983)
- Geometric primitives + dynamically redefinable character sets (DRCS)
- Herb Bown (CRC lead) — Vista project ($10M Bell Canada deal, 1979; abandoned 1983)
- Alex terminal service (1988–94)

### 11. RIPscrip
**Sources:**
- en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_Imaging_Protocol
- BBS Documentary Library — bbsdocumentary.com/library/PROGRAMS/GRAPHICS/RIPSCRIPT/
- Break Into Chat RIP Wiki — breakintochat.com/wiki/Remote_Imaging_Protocol_(RIP)
- GitHub RIPterm.js emulator — github.com/cgorringe/RIPtermJS (2024)

**Coverage:**
- TeleGrafix Communications (Huntington Beach, CA, 1992–98)
- RIPterm client software archives
- RIPscrip 1.5x — most common in production
- 2.0 / 3.0 — planned but unfulfilled
- Creators: Jeff Reeder, Jim Bergman, Mark Hayton
- Serion BBS — RIP-enabled server still running 2024

## Tier 3 — Modern / Contemporary

### 12. Lovebyte Demoparty (Sizecoding)
**URL:** https://lovebyte.party/
**2025 event:** demoparty.net/lovebyte/lovebyte-2025 (Feb 15–16, Discord + Twitch)

**Coverage:**
- Annual 256-character art compos: ANSI / PETSCII / ATASCII / Teletext
- Tiny Code Christmas (Dec 2024) — newcomer onboarding event

**2025 winners:**
- harvest — *Random Access Byte ANSI*, #22
- Imagician/BionFX — *Tiny Tower Toppler ANSI*, 1st place

Focus: constraint-based innovation. Lovebyte is the single most-important contemporary ANSI venue.

### 13. Christopher Johnson's ASCII Art Collection

**Coverage as of 2026:**

- 30 years of continuous ASCII preservation

Modern aggregator + creation tooling.

**Updates (2026):**
- Account system launched March 8, 2026
- Cloud storage + favorites + community sharing

## Tier 4 — Defunct Reconstruction (Wayback Machine)

### Defunct Artgroup Websites
**Search strategy:**
- `"ACiD productions" site:web.archive.org` 2000–2005 snapshots
- `"iCE advertisements" site:web.archive.org` 1994–1998
- ACiD's 1992–1996 group websites (Wayback)
- iCE's 1991–1995 BBS advertisements (textfiles.com mirror)

Typical findings: group declaration NFO files reconstructed as HTML, ASCII art embedded in early web pages, member lists, release schedules.

### Usenet alt.ascii-art
**URL:** https://groups.google.com/g/alt.ascii-art

**Status — CRITICAL LOSS:** Google Groups severed Usenet NNTP gateway **February 22, 2024**. The 30+ year discussion archive (1980s–Feb 2024) is now read-only. No new posts. Deja.com (dejanews acquisition, 2001) → 404.

**Implications:** Documenting community discourse on ASCII art technique post-2024 requires alternative venues — Mastodon, Discord, Lovebyte forums.

## Niche Specialized Format Archives

### 16. Tundra Format
**Source:** justsolve.archiveteam.org/wiki/TUNDRA

TUNDRA24 format (control-A, -B, -D, -F codes). Alternative to ANSI. Primarily 1980s–90s demos.

Ansilove converter (github.com/ansilove/ansilove) supports Tundra font (2016+).

### 17. LambdaMOO / Talker Archives
- MOOcows archive (Apr 1990s–2010s)
- TinyMUD documentation
- Text-based virtual world art/design documents
- MOO code tutorials including text-based art generation
- Avatar descriptions as ASCII art

LambdaMOO still online; sparse activity.

### 18. ATASCII (Atari 8-bit)
**Primary:**
- atariarchives.org
- atarimagazines.com
- ASCII Art Atari category at asciiart.eu
- Atari Age Forums (active "New ATASCII art?" threads)

**Coverage:**
- Classic Computer Magazine Archive (Antic, STart, Creative Computing, Compute!)
- Iconverter — ATASCII art conversion tutorials
- Atari 400/800 (1979)
- Dedicated Atari ANSI hobbyist community (smaller than PETSCII but active)

### 19. Tundra / Avatar / Wildcat formats
Less-canonical BBS art formats. Found across textfiles.com / scene.org / 16colo.rs / Defacto2 cross-references.

### Telnet BBS Directory
**URL:** https://www.telnetbbsguide.com/

**Status:** 1,028+ BBS listed as of 2024. Includes:
- Contemporary BBS sysops commissioning new ANSI art
- Deco screens + game interfaces designed for 2024-era hardware
- Atari BBS listing: http://sfhqbbs.org/ataribbslist.php (active 2026)
- RIPscrip-enabled BBS: Serion BBS
- Continuous ANSI-decorated systems

**369 relevance:** ANSI art is not a historical artifact. It is an *active medium* with a small but dedicated 2026 community.
