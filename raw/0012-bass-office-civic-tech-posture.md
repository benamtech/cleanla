# LA Mayor's Office (Bass Admin) Civic-Tech Posture 2026

> Compiled 2026-05-24 for CleanLA's post-CLWM-validation Mayor's-office activation play.

## Executive summary

The Bass administration prioritizes grassroots community engagement in municipal services, as evidenced by the Mayor's public recognition of Juan Naula's Clean LA With Me in State of the City addresses (2024, State unclear but confirmed). Inside Safe, her signature homelessness initiative, is resource-heavy ($322M spent) but results-challenged (only 1,243 in permanent housing; 40% recidivism). The city recently modernized MyLA311 citizen-reporting app (launch March 2026) and maintains open-data infrastructure (GeoHub, ITA under Ted Ross). Rachel Brashier, appointed Deputy Chief of Staff for City Services (January 2026), brings deep LA government experience and directs cross-departmental service-delivery coordination—a critical gatekeeper for civic-tech pilots. The window is small: Bass faces re-election in June 2026, and LA28 Olympics infrastructure spending will dominate 2026–2028 budgeting. CleanLA's strongest pitch levers are neighborhood coverage, grassroots validation, privacy-first framing (aligns with Dignity-first Inside Safe tone), and data partnership potential.

## Bass administration priorities relevant to CleanLA (2024–2026)

### Inside Safe (Housing-First Homelessness Response)
- **What it is:** Mayor Bass's flagship initiative to address street homelessness through rapid encampment operations and transitional-housing placement with wraparound services.
- **Performance (as of 2026-05-24):**
  - 5,179 people served across 114 encampment operations.
  - Only 1,243 in permanent housing; 1,636 in interim placements.
  - 40% of exited participants back on streets (recidivism crisis).
  - Total spend: $322M+ (2022–2026).
  - Bass's original 2022 promise: "end street homelessness by 2026." Status: 17.5% reduction achieved, not 100%.
  - **Key tone:** Housing-first, less enforcement-focused than predecessor. Emphasis on dignity, wraparound services, coordinated entry.
  - **Why relevant to CleanLA:** Bass's posture is NOT "enforcement + sweep + displace." It's "coordinate+house+restore." CleanLA's privacy-first, dignity-first reporting framing aligns visibly.
- **Leadership:** No specific program director identified in public sources. Program operates under Mayor's direct office + LAHSA (Los Angeles Homeless Services Authority).

### Shine LA (Community Beautification Initiative)
- **What it is:** City-wide cleanup/beautification movement launched April 2025 (tied to State of the City address announcement).
- **Scale:** 2,000+ Angelenos across 30+ neighborhoods since April 2025. Monthly on fourth Saturday.
- **Examples:** KYCC (Koreatown), Pacoima Beautiful, tree-planting, graffiti removal, litter pickup, composting education.
- **Tone:** Community-led, partner-heavy (nonprofits + small businesses + city departments). Civic participation framing, not government mandate.
- **Why relevant to CleanLA:** Shine LA demonstrates Bass's appetite for grassroots cleanup partnerships. It's not a city program; it's a city + community co-production model. This is CleanLA's path.

### Bureau of Sanitation / StreetsLA Initiatives
- **Street Sweeping 2026:** Updated schedule released December 2025. Specific details not publicly available; check streets.lacity.gov.
- **Fee Structure:** Monthly single-family trash fee increased from $36.32 → $55.94 (January 2026).
- **Clean Streets Index:** City maintains data-driven street-cleanliness scoring (1–3 per street), visualized publicly.
- **Why relevant to CleanLA:** City tracks street cleanliness as a metric. CleanLA's reporting data could feed into public scorecard, creating feedback loop and partnership value.

### A Bridge Home (Interim Homelessness Housing, Pre-Bass)
- **Context:** Started under Mayor Garcetti (2018). 15 temporary emergency housing facilities planned (one per council district), 1,500 beds.
- **Outcomes:** Sites opened; low throughput to permanent housing. Initial 3-month target missed; residents stay 18+ months.
- **Status under Bass:** Continued but deprioritized in favor of Inside Safe.
- **Why relevant to CleanLA:** Demonstrates city's track record with phased, neighborhood-by-neighborhood pilots. Template for scaling.

## Existing civic-tech partnerships and precedents (Bass admin, 2022–2026)

### MyLA311 (City-Owned Citizen Reporting)
- **What:** Evolved citizen-service-request system, rebuilt March 2025 by 3Di Systems. Allows residents to report potholes, graffiti, bulky-item pickups, illegal dumping, etc. "Drop a pin" feature for inexact addresses (alleys, LA River, etc.).
- **Model:** City-owned, vendor-managed (3Di). Built with resident, neighborhood-council, and business feedback.
- **Relevance:** Shows city's openness to digital service channels. Potential integration point for CleanLA data (cross-reporting, data partnerships).
- **Sources:** [MyLA311 official site](https://lacity.gov/myla311); [CBS coverage](https://www.cbsnews.com/losangeles/news/los-angeles-city-service-myla311-app-site/).

### LA GeoHub (Open-Data Infrastructure)
- **What:** City + Esri partnership. Public portal for location-based city data, APIs available to developers, nonprofits, academics.
- **Model:** City provides data, opens APIs; private sector builds. No formal endorsement required.
- **Relevance:** CleanLA could register data products on GeoHub, positioning as civic-tech partner (not contractor).
- **Source:** [Esri newsroom](https://www.esri.com/about/newsroom/arcwatch/los-angeles-opens-geohub-a-blueprint-for-smart-communities/).

### PledgeLA (Tech-Sector Diversity Initiative)
- **What:** Partnership between Annenberg Foundation, Mayor's office, venture capital, and tech leaders. Launched 2018 (Garcetti era). Tracks diversity in VC funding, expands nonprofit engagement with tech sector.
- **Status under Bass:** Appears to continue (LA500 2026 naming Bass).
- **Relevance:** Not a civic-tech partnership per se, but shows city's comfort with tech-sector co-production on social outcomes.
- **Source:** [PNDonline](https://philanthropynewsdigest.org/news/los-angeles-annenberg-launch-tech-sector-civic-initiative).

### Civic Tech USC / USC Annenberg Research
- **What:** USC Annenberg School hosts Civic Tech USC research center. Releases reports on open data, civic engagement, and tech in LA.
- **Model:** Academic partnership, not formal city endorsement, but cited in city strategy.
- **Relevance:** Model for credibility: university-backed civic-tech research lends legitimacy to grassroots tools.

### NO Major Endorsements of Hack for LA or Code for America Brigade
- **Finding:** Web search for "Code for LA OR Hack for LA" + "Bass administration" returned no direct partnerships.
- **Implication:** Door is open. Bass has not formally adopted a civic-tech platform yet. CleanLA could be first.

## What the Mayor's office actually buys (artifacts + numbers for civic-tech pitches)

Based on Bass administration's public statements, Shine LA, and Inside Safe communications, these proof points move them:

### Numbers That Work
1. **Volunteer hours mobilized.** (Shine LA: 2,000+ Angelenos, 30+ neighborhoods since April 2025 = ~24 months of monthly engagement.)
2. **Geographic coverage & specificity.** (Street-by-street data, neighborhood-level rollup, not aggregates. Clean Streets Index model.)
3. **Cost per outcome.** (Inside Safe: $322M for 5,179 served = $62K/person. CleanLA's volunteer + tech model = vastly lower cost-per-report-filed.)
4. **Recidivism / retention metrics.** (Inside Safe's 40% recidivism is painful. CleanLA can show repeat-reporter engagement, suggesting sustained community buy-in.)
5. **Replicability across districts.** (Shine LA's monthly rollout across neighborhoods shows city values scalable, district-by-district activation.)

### Artifacts That Work
1. **Before/after photos of specific neighborhoods.** (Shine LA events heavily documented this way.)
2. **Volunteer story profiles.** (Meet Naula, meet other CLWM organizers. Bass's SOTC strategy centers human stories.)
3. **Press clips already in hand.** (Naula's media profile means CleanLA comes with earned media, not just hope.)
4. **Data partnership mockup.** (Show how CleanLA reports → MyLA311 integration OR GeoHub dataset. City loves open-data framing.)
5. **Endorsement chain.** (Clean LA With Me → CleanLA tool → Naula's validated-use case → Mayor's endorsement. Linear story.)

### Stories That Work
- **"Grassroots + city co-production."** (Not "we're disrupting city services." Rather: "we're making volunteers' work visible + scalable.")
- **"Dignity in data."** (Privacy-first reporting, no surveillance camera integration, no targeting of homeless encampments. Aligns with Inside Safe's tone.)
- **"Bridging Shine LA energy + permanent data.** (Shine LA events are monthly, episodic. CleanLA makes ongoing volunteer energy legible to city planners.)
- **"Olympic legacy."** (LA28 infrastructure push; LA28 also demands public-space improvements; CleanLA as one tool in broader "clean, welcoming LA" narrative.)

## Inside Safe / Encampment-Cleanup Tone — How CleanLA Aligns

**Bass's Posture:**
- Housing-first, not enforcement-first.
- Emphasis on dignity, wraparound services, coordinated entry.
- Critique from left: not enough housing + not enough services.
- Critique from right: still too soft, recidivism too high.
- **Bass's response:** Framing as "emergency operations with compassion," tie to LA28 + "welcoming city" narrative.

**CleanLA's Alignment:**
1. **Privacy-first data collection.** CleanLA doesn't target individuals; it reports infrastructure (potholes, illegal dumping, graffiti). No encampment mapping or individual surveillance.
2. **Volunteer-dignified framing.** Naula's language: "service," "love," community co-production. Not "you are the problem."
3. **Positive framing.** CleanLA is about "making beautiful neighborhoods," not "clearing the streets of undesirables."
4. **Data for service planning, not enforcement.** City can use CleanLA reports to deploy sanitation (garbage pickup, pressure washing), not LAPD.

**Pitch Language:**
> "CleanLA amplifies the volunteer energy Bass celebrates in Shine LA. By making community-reported street issues visible in real time, CleanLA helps the city prioritize sanitation services in the neighborhoods most in need—a dignity-first approach that aligns with Inside Safe's housing-first philosophy and supports LA28's public-space readiness."

## LA28 Olympics Tie-In

### Why It Matters
- **Timeline:** LA28 happens summer 2028. Budget pressure starts 2026–2027.
- **Spending:** $1B+ federal funding (security, planning); Metro's $3.3B Olympic project list, though only 5.2% funded as of late 2024; Congress approved $94.3M for mobility hubs, transit, pedestrian access (Feb 2026).
- **Procurement:** LA28 aims 75% of addressable spend to Greater LA region, 25% to small businesses.
- **Civic narrative:** LA28 as opportunity to "showcase" LA, improve public spaces, demonstrate city innovation.

### CleanLA's LA28 Angle
1. **"Clean city" branding.** LA28 wants global media to see "LA at its best." Volunteer-powered street cleanliness supports that narrative.
2. **Neighborhood-level readiness.** CleanLA could pilot in Olympic-adjacent neighborhoods (Boyle Heights → DTLA proximity). Data can be packaged as "Olympic legacy" infrastructure readiness.
3. **Tech innovation story.** LA28 procurement includes digital/civic-tech components. CleanLA could be positioned as homegrown LA civic-tech (vs. out-of-state corporate solutions).
4. **Accessibility to non-tech residents.** Volunteers using CleanLA = inclusive tech adoption story for diversity-focused LA28 narrative.
