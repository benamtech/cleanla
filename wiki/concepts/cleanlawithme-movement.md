A registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit and hashtag-driven cleanup movement in Los Angeles, founded by Juan Eduardo Naula in November 2024. By early 2026, the most natural distribution partner for any LA-focused civic cleanup app — including [[../projects/cleanla-snap]].

## Origin

Naula, an Ecuadorian native, moved to LA from Virginia in October 2024 to develop a rideshare app with an LA-based investor. He was scammed out of $8,000 in his first two weeks. He started cleaning streets in November 2024 as therapy. His wife told him not to leave.

## Scale (early 2026, approximate)

- ~69K Instagram followers (@cleanlawithme)
- ~45K Facebook followers
- 100,000+ pounds of trash hauled
- 1,500+ filled bags
- Daily TikTok/Instagram content showing before/after of specific LA blocks
- Built physical receptacle stations (compost / trash / cans / batteries) in Boyle Heights; plans to expand
- Quit his hardwood flooring day job to do this full-time; relies on donations + GoFundMe

## Operating model

- Daily solo cleanups documented as short-form video
- Saturday volunteer meetups in rotating neighborhoods, coordinated via Instagram DMs
- Hashtag mechanic (#CleanLAwithMe) explicitly modeled on the ice bucket challenge: users post their own cleanup videos using the tag
- "Show up, clean, post, repeat" — content formula optimized for short-form video
- Pairs activism with critique of city government: *"I started cleaning because the city wouldn't"*
- Calls for systemic change: education programs on waste disposal, permanent neighborhood cleanup stations

## Friction

- Two hit-and-run collisions damaged his vehicle (active fundraising for a pickup truck)
- Donation-dependent; limited equipment
- No technology platform — purely social media + IRL meetups

## Why it matters to CleanLA Snap

- His audience IS the natural user base — already engaged, already documenting cleanups, already trained on the before/after format the app's data model needs.
- His "before/after" content format maps directly onto the app's status-change feature (Open → Cleaned with photo proof).
- A volunteer-event feature in the app could replace the ad-hoc Instagram-DM coordination he relies on for Saturday meetups.
- Aggregated report data (heatmaps of high-trash blocks) directly supports his city-partnership ambition for permanent receptacle stations.
- Aligns with the [[snapcrap-case-study]] launch playbook: piggyback on an existing engaged audience instead of building one from zero.

## Open questions

- Is Naula open to a formal partnership (e.g., app branded "Clean LA With Me — official reporting tool")?
- Are there 501(c)(3) constraints on the nonprofit endorsing a tech product?
- Could the app be operated *by* the 501(c)(3) directly, solving [[civic-app-patterns-and-failure-modes]]'s "founder-burnout collapse" problem from day one?
