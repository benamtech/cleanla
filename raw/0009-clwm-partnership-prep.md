# Clean LA With Me (CLWM) — Partnership-Prep Deep-Dive (2026)

> Compiled 2026-05-24 for CleanLA's Phase 1.5 partnership conversation with Juan Naula. Target: 30-60 min call within 14 days (by 2026-06-07). Goal: walk in informed and frame the ask strategically.

## Executive summary

Juan Eduardo Naula, a Venezuelan-born former flooring installer from Virginia, launched Clean LA With Me as a viral grassroots nonprofit after arriving in LA 14–18 months ago. The org mobilizes 250+ residents per cleanup cycle on a weekly cadence across multiple neighborhoods (Boyle Heights, East LA, DTLA areas confirmed). With ~50K Instagram followers and recent press recognition from ABC7, NBC LA, FOX11, iHeart, Washington Post, and LA Public Press, CLWM operates on a lean donation model ($10K Guinness Record fundraising goal) and uses Google Forms + Instagram for volunteer recruitment. Mayor Karen Bass publicly endorsed Naula's work. No evidence of corporate partnerships or municipal integration yet; significant opportunity for tech tool integration sits in current Instagram/Google Forms workflow. Reputational profile is clean — positioned as dignity-forward, humanitarian helper (documented interactions with homeless individuals), not enforcement-adjacent.

> **Discrepancy note:** the CleanLA wiki's `[[cleanlawithme-movement]]` previously cited ~69K followers; this research found ~50K. Either the wiki figure was stale, or the count dropped between snapshots, or the count is volatile. Verify in the call.

## Hints at unmet tech needs

**Direct quote (from cleanlawithme.org vision):**
- "By 2026: 100+ permanent cleanup hubs with tools and local leadership"
- "By 2030: Smart waste infrastructure with AI-powered systems to reduce landfill usage"

This suggests Naula is thinking about:
- **Decentralization:** Moving beyond solo cleanup to neighborhood-based hubs
- **Local leadership model:** Training volunteer coordinators, not just volunteers
- **Infrastructure integration:** Smart systems, data tracking, municipal scale
- **Sustainability tech:** AI, landfill reduction optimization

**Inferred pain points:**
1. **Volunteer coordination:** Single Google Form doesn't scale to 100+ hubs; need neighborhood team organization, shift scheduling, role matching
2. **Impact tracking:** "1,500 bags" is manual counting; no structured data on cleanup locations, types of waste, trends
3. **Donor/volunteer journey:** Giving Circles and volunteer recruitment are disconnected; no unified CRM
4. **Municipal credibility:** To partner with city, needs standardized reporting, GIS mapping, outcome documentation
5. **Scale coordination:** Without tools, can't manage multiple concurrent cleanups or neighborhood hubs

**Not explicitly stated but contextually urgent:**
- Naula suffered TWO hit-and-runs while cleaning (August 2025) — safety/insurance implications; lack of organized accountability for cleanup locations
- Volunteer liability: No formal waiver system, no incident reporting structure visible

## Recommended ask / offer / questions for the call

### OFFER (frame these early):

1. **Volunteer coordination platform:** "We can help you organize your weekly cleanups into neighborhood-based hubs. Instead of a Google Form, you get a hub management tool where volunteers sign up for specific teams, dates, roles — and you can track who's a repeat volunteer, who might lead a neighborhood hub."

2. **Impact data layer:** "Every cleanup generates data — trash types, locations, weight. Right now it's manual. We can give you live cleanup mapping, trend reporting, and exportable impact reports for donors and the city."

3. **Donor-volunteer integration:** "Your Giving Circles and volunteer pipeline are separate. We can unify them — so a volunteer becomes a donor, a donor becomes a volunteer, and you see the full supporter journey."

4. **Scaling to 100+ hubs:** "Your 2026 goal is 100+ cleanup hubs with local leadership. Our tool can decentralize that — each neighborhood hub gets its own team, leaders, mini-dashboards, without you managing everything from one Instagram account."

### ASK (learn his actual needs first):

1. **Hub scaling:** "You mention 100 hubs by 2026. What does that look like operationally? Are you training neighborhood coordinators? How do you see volunteers organizing without you personally leading every cleanup?"

2. **Donor accountability:** "Your donors give $10K Guinness campaigns. How do they know what happened to their money? Do you track dumpster costs, supplies, volunteer hours? What would make donors more confident they're funding sustained impact?"

3. **City integration:** "Mayor Bass endorsed you. Have you explored formal partnership with the Bureau of Street Services or Sanitation? What's blocking it? (Data? Liability? Political will?)"

4. **Volunteer retention:** "You have 250+ per event. How many repeat volunteers do you have? How do you keep people engaged long-term vs. one-off cleanup?"

5. **Tech philosophy:** "You're already using Google Forms, Instagram, Giving Circles. If we proposed a more integrated tool, what's your hesitation? Cost? Control? Over-professionalization?"

### AVOID (don't lead with these):

1. **"Your current setup is broken"** — CLWM is viral and working socially. Don't insult the grassroots model.
2. **"We'll help you go corporate / get a board / hire staff"** — Not your role, and he may reject professionalization. Stay in lane: tools for organizing, not org structure consulting.
3. **Corporate partnerships / sponsorship** — Zero appetite shown in research. Don't pitch logos, branding, or corporate volunteers.
4. **Encampment clearing / homeless enforcement** — Explicitly avoid. Naula's brand is dignity-first, not cleanup-as-sweeping.
5. **Political alignment** — Don't ask about Mayor Bass ties, city council politics, or Newsom's homeless strategy. Naula's staying apolitical; respect that.
6. **Data extraction / surveillance optics** — If CleanLA proposes tracking individual volunteers, unhoused people in areas, or sharing data with city agencies without explicit consent language, Naula will reject it. Frame data as CLWM-owned, not city-controlled.
