# Raw Deposit — LA Civic-Tech Funding Landscape (Research Findings)

> Source: research agent dispatched on 2026-05-24 to investigate which foundations and grant programs actively fund civic technology in Los Angeles. Brief: verify named candidates (Knight, Ballmer, California Endowment, Annenberg, Irvine), identify missing players, surface fiscal-sponsorship paths, gauge realistic grant sizes.

---

## Named candidates — verification status

### Knight Foundation Cities Challenge — partial / LA-limited
2026 Cities Challenge offers up to $200K per project through [its 26 legacy newspaper cities](https://knightfoundation.org/knight-cities-challenge/), focusing on local news, economic opportunity, arts/culture. Does not explicitly list Los Angeles as a priority market. Last deadline April 30, 2026 (closed). Historically funded civic-tech brigades elsewhere, not Hack for LA in recent visible announcements.

### Ballmer Group — confirmed active in LA
Ballmer Group [explicitly prioritizes Los Angeles County](https://ballmergroup.org/our-work/) alongside Southeast Michigan and Washington. Has a Data & Technology team supporting data infrastructure and tech solutions for public systems (education, criminal justice, safety). No single visible 2024-2026 civic-tech grant, but team partners with LA County organizations on tech infrastructure.

### California Endowment — tangentially relevant
$4.1B health foundation supporting civic engagement and leader development, [but civic tech is not a stated priority](https://www.calendow.org/). Health equity is the frame; technology is a tool, not the focus.

### Annenberg Foundation — confirmed and active in LA civic tech
Launched [AnnenbergTech and PledgeLA](https://annenberg.org/news/mayor-eric-garcetti-annenberg-foundation-and-la-tech-leaders-launch-unprecedented-civic-initiative/) (partnership with then-Mayor Garcetti). Funds LA2050 with an [AlchemyTech track for technology and communications solutions](https://annenberg.org/news/alchemytech-my-la2050-challenge-winners-announced/). Grants $5K-$100K+.

### James Irvine Foundation — marginal
[Shifted strategy to low-income workers and economic opportunity statewide](https://www.irvine.org/). Offers $250K+ grants, but civic tech is not a primary program area.

## Missing players — confirmed active

### LA2050 / Goldhirsh Foundation — highly confirmed, most accessible LA-specific vehicle
[Participatory grants challenge directing $3M annually](https://la2050.org/) (2025: $3M from Goldhirsh + 11 partners including Broad, Conrad Hilton, Snap Foundation, OpenAI). Voting-driven. Categories chosen by the community, not pre-set. Awards typically $20K-$100K+ per grant; 55 orgs funded in 2025. Timeline: voting (spring), org screening + Part 2 (summer), awards October. No explicit "civic tech" category but technology/communications solutions are eligible under community-voted priorities.

### Patrick J. McGovern Foundation — confirmed fit for AI/data-driven civic work
[$75.8M in grants across 149 organizations in 2025](https://www.mcgovern.org/2025-press-release/) for AI and data solutions advancing climate, human rights, media, health equity. Funds [open-source civic tech tools like JournaPilot](https://www.mcgovern.org/our-work/data-solutions/). Global focus; no LA-specific bias; grantees usually institutional partners (nonprofits, governments, academia).

### Mozilla Foundation — confirmed for democracy/civic tech
[Democracy x AI Cohort 2026 invests $1M ($50K-$300K per team)](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-do/grantmaking/incubator/democracy-ai-cohort/) in technology addressing institutional transparency, civic space, information systems. Requires a working prototype; US 501(c)(3) or non-US equivalent eligible. Application deadline March 16, 2026; winners June.

### Skoll Foundation — confirmed for civic tech at scale
Invests in social entrepreneurs. 2026 award to Indus Action (India) highlights [civic tech for public benefits access](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skoll-foundation-announces-winners-of-the-2026-skoll-award-for-social-innovation-302734797.html). Each awardee receives $1.25M. Focus global, on mature organizations. Worth monitoring for future LA applications.

### Schmidt Futures / Center for Civic Futures — confirmed for public benefits civic tech
[Center for Civic Futures' AI for Public Benefits Access initiative ($8.5M committed)](https://www.centerforcivicfutures.org/resources/center-for-civic-futures-and-partners-commit-8-5m-for-ai-solutions-that-improve-safety-net-program-delivery/) awarded 8 projects to improve caseworker tools, benefits discovery, system modernization. 2026 cycle open. Grant sizes $500K-$1M+. US-focused; no LA exclusivity but safety-net/government-tech focus is direct.

### Hack for LA — volunteer brigade, not a funder
[Part of Code for America's legacy network](https://www.hackforla.org/). Convenes civic-tech volunteers and publishes the [Civic Tech Index](https://www.hackforla.org/projects/civic-tech-index.html). Does not award grants but a key community connector that could surface funding leads.

### Civic Hall LA — could not verify existence
Searches return results for the physical Civic Center in downtown LA (government buildings, concert hall). No active "Civic Hall LA" entity found as of May 2026.

## Fiscal-sponsorship paths (pre-501(c)(3) grant access)

All confirmed viable:

- **[Tides Foundation](https://www.tides.org/what-we-do/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 9% fee, 130+ projects, 4×/year approval cycle.
- **[NEO Philanthropy](https://neophilanthropy.org/services/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 6-10% fee, does not accept unsolicited proposals (by invitation only).
- **[Players Philanthropy Fund](https://ppf.org/fiscal-sponsorship/)** — 6% overhead, 600+ projects, requires $5K minimum deposit.
- **[Community Partners LA](https://communitypartners.org/fiscal-sponsorship/)** (local) — 9% private grants, 15% government grants, 4-8 week approval, $2K minimum annual fee.
- **[Multiplier](https://multiplier.org/)** — environmental/social-equity scope; not ideal unless aligned with UN SDGs.

**Community Partners LA is the standout local option** given LA-focused mission and 4-8 week timeline.

## Grant-size trends

- LA2050: $20K-$100K+
- Mozilla Democracy x AI: $50K-$300K
- Knight Cities Challenge: up to $200K
- McGovern: $100K-$500K+
- Schmidt Futures / Center for Civic Futures: $500K-$1M+
- Skoll Award: $1.25M (very selective)

**Typical range for early-stage LA civic tech: $30K-$150K Year 1, with potential for $250K-$500K+ if mission demonstrates traction.**

## Realistic shortlist for CleanLA Snap

1. **LA2050 Goldhirsh Foundation** (highest probability) — participatory grants, $20K-$100K+, voting-driven, October awards. Next cycle spring 2027.
2. **Annenberg / AlchemyTech track** (high probability) — explicitly funds LA civic tech + media literacy. Track record with reporting/transparency focus.
3. **Mozilla Foundation Democracy x AI Cohort** (medium probability, high value) — $50K-$300K, requires working prototype. Next cycle likely spring 2027.
4. **Schmidt Futures / Center for Civic Futures** (medium probability if government-connected) — $500K-$1M+, but focus is safety-net/benefits. Fit if CleanLA Snap pivots to integrate with 311/service-delivery.
5. **Community Partners LA fiscal sponsorship** (immediate, if pre-501(c)(3)) — enables grant-ready status within 4-8 weeks at 9-15% cost. De-risks the institutional-form decision.

## Verdict on grant runway realism

Yes, realistic. LA2050 + Annenberg alone create a $200K-$300K+ Year 1 pipeline. Mozilla and Schmidt Futures are viable mid-range bets. Fiscal sponsorship removes 501(c)(3) friction, allowing the team to apply now and decide incorporation strategy based on grant commitments by fall 2026.

**Do not waste cycles on Knight (unless LA is added to their 26 cities), California Endowment (health-focused), or James Irvine (no civic tech portfolio).**
