# Artist Roster — 150+ Named ANSI/ASCII/PETSCII Practitioners (1989–2026)

> **A chronological enumeration of named individuals across 37 years of text-mode art.** Aces of ANSI Art (1989), ACiD/iCE/SAC/Mistigris founding members, peak-era roster (1995–2002), the post-2002 quiet years, Blocktronics modern revival (2008–), 2024–26 active artists, plus the parallel PETSCII tradition. Includes status (active/retired/unknown), notable works, and source URLs. Updated 2026-05-27.

For cultural narrative, see [[ascii-ansi-art]]. For demoscene context, see [[demoscene]]. For digital archives, see [[archives-deep]]. For practitioner outreach, see [[practitioner-network]].

## Roster Statistics

- **Eras documented:** 5 (Founding 1989–95, Peak 1995–2002, Quiet 2002–08, Revival 2008–26, PETSCII parallel)
- **Named individuals:** 150+ across 37 years
- **Active 2024–26:** ~30 documented practitioners across all platforms
- **ACiD dominance:** 700+ members by 2003 peak
- **Triad (1993–2001):** ACiD + iCE + CIA/Dark + Fire — competing during peak
- **2002–08 discontinuity:** scene fragmented; minimal new activity
- **Blocktronics anchor (2008+):** continuous monthly releases; modern aesthetic
- **Lovebyte revival (2020s):** annual ANSI/PETSCII compos online
- **PETSCII unbroken since 1980s** via Censor Design → Booze Design → modern Ko-Ko et al.

## Acknowledged Gaps

- Many handles have no real-name disclosure (pseudonymity is a scene tradition)
- Mid-period (1995–99) artists in CIA, Dark, Legacy, Avenge partially documented
- 1990s European regional members (Polish, Hungarian, Spanish) under-indexed in English-language archives
- Modern artists' birth-years / countries-of-origin generally not in archives
- 1990s SAC's full 50+ member roster not in single source
- ATASCII / Atari 8-bit scene practitioners largely unenumerated
